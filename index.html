Executing project is all about being industry ready

Executing project is all about knowing how experts execute the project. Which tools and technologies to be used. Applying the knowledge you gain over the years to this one problem in hand.

Taking all those design designs and knowing why you are taking them. Its about carefully weighing each option and choosing the optimal one. Its about collaborating with the team and adding value.

If you still code, test, build individually on your local code-base, you may not be ready for industry yet.

Collaborate

Projects, even college projects are developed in a group for a reason. You need to learn how to collaborate with the team and add value. No individual can win this game by himself. A big part of your professional success would come from listening to others. Even to those who you think, know less stuff than you.

Work Together

Never work too long on a local code-base. Industry uses source code management system like git, project management tools like jira, build automations tools like jenkins to avoid a lot of issue they discovered over the years. Most students and eventually projects suffer a lot as junior developers lack basic understanding of these tools.

Continuous Improvement

Waterfall days are gone. Everyone now wants to see, track and measure their progress over time. You need to be able to learn estimation and breaking down tasks into SMART user stories. During project execution, you need to accommodate changes and deal with them effectively.

First Time Right

You do not have time to make all the mistakes by yourself. Talk to experts and understand their take on certain hot trends. They can tell you all about their hard earned learning and issues that kept them awake though nights. You can get benefit and use this knowledge to excel in your carrear path

Impress

Technical interviews are now tougher than ever. Panelist do not have time and patience to understand your project and then ask questions based on that complex algorithm you have implemented. They may not have heard of the IEEE white paper your project is based on. Show them demo on your smart phone and share your code on your github repository. Impress them with your project presentation deployed on cloud.

Frequently Asked Questions

There are so many questions students ask before and while doing their final year project. But more important are those question which they do not ask. I have tried to mention few below

Which area/domain should I chose for my project?

Its not really about the domain but how many skills you can showcase in that area. Certain experimental technologies look fancy but I would not recommend them for final year project. Like deep learning, machine learning, AI, block-chain. You concept should not be too complex that it overshadow simpler things you learn while doing a actual project.

Which technology should I use?

I always prefer open stack for various reasons. You should be able to demonstrate your application at real time. It should be intuitive and simple to understand. I would prefer hiring a student who has done Library management system really well, than a student who has worked on digital signal processing and pattern recognition but would not demonstrate it

My college needs research paper published in last year for any topic to qualify, what should I do?

There are plenty of research paper published on UX, performance improvement and cloud hosting. Try to fit in your idea in those papers instead of choosing a random topic just because you saw a research paper on it.

I have to have login page in my flow to authenticate my user, right?

Not really, you should integrate with google or facebook for authentication. Most new applications do that in production. Besides who has patience to remember a different user-name and password for every site they visit.
